Elephant Mountain (Xiangshan)

Short hike, big reward — the classic postcard view of Taipei 101 and the city skyline.

About Elephant Mountain

Introduction

Elephant Mountain, known locally as Xiangshan, is one of the most popular urban hiking trails in Taipei. Just a short walk from the city streets, this hill offers spectacular views over Taipei 101, the Xinyi skyline, and the surrounding mountains.

The hike itself is relatively short but quite steep, consisting mostly of stone stairs. Most visitors reach the main viewing platforms in about 15–25 minutes, making it an easy add-on to a day spent around Taipei 101 or the Xinyi District.

The Trail and Viewpoints

The most common starting point is near Xiangshan MRT Station (Red Line). From Exit 2, it takes about 10 minutes to reach the trailhead, where a series of stone steps leads you up through forested slopes and pavilions.

Along the way, several viewing decks and rest areas offer chances to catch your breath and enjoy the scenery. The most famous spots are the viewing platforms and large boulders near the main lookout, where many of the iconic Taipei 101 photos are taken.

From Elephant Mountain, more experienced hikers can continue on to neighboring peaks such as Tiger Mountain, Leopard Mountain, and Lion Mountain as part of the Four Beasts trail system, but most casual visitors are happy to stop at the main viewpoints.

Hiking Highlights

Main Trail & Stairs

The standard Elephant Mountain trail is short but steep, consisting largely of well-maintained stone steps. Most people with average fitness can reach the main viewpoints in 15–25 minutes, but you should still expect to break a sweat.

There are railings along much of the path, and benches or pavilions where you can stop to rest. The trail is family-friendly, though small children and people with knee issues may find the stairs challenging.

Viewpoints & Photo Spots

Several viewing platforms along the trail offer different angles of Taipei 101 and the city. The most famous spots are near the top, where raised decks and large rocks create iconic vantage points.

These areas can get crowded at sunset and on weekends, especially with tripods and photographers setting up for long-exposure shots of the city lights. If you want a good photo spot, arrive early and be prepared to share space with others.

Best Time & Night Views

The most popular time to visit Elephant Mountain is around sunset, when you can see the city gradually light up as the sky changes color. On clear days, you get both daylight and night views in a single visit.

Evening hikes are very common; the trail is equipped with lights, and you’ll rarely be alone. Still, good shoes and a small flashlight or phone light are helpful, especially for the steps on the way down.

Visiting Tips

How to Make the Most of Your Visit

Wear comfortable shoes and bring water, especially in the warmer months—Taipei can be hot and humid, and the stairs feel much longer when you are dehydrated. A small towel or tissues are handy if you tend to sweat easily.

Try to plan around the weather: clear days offer the best visibility, while heavy rain can make the steps slippery and views disappointing. On summer afternoons, consider starting later in the day to avoid the strongest sun, and use sunblock or a hat.

Many visitors combine Elephant Mountain with a visit to Taipei 101, since both are located in or near the Xinyi District. Hike for the view first, then head down for dinner or shopping in the city below.

Practical Information

  • Location: Xinyi District, Taipei City (south-east of Taipei 101).
  • Nearest MRT: Xiangshan Station (Red Line), usually Exit 2. Follow the signs toward the trailhead.
  • Trail Length: Around 1.5 km for a basic up-and-down visit to the main viewpoints.
  • Difficulty: Short but steep; mostly stairs. Suitable for most visitors with reasonable fitness.
  • Opening Hours: The trail is generally accessible 24/7, but lighting is better in the early evening.
  • Suggested Time: About 1.5–2 hours including hiking, photos, and resting at the top.
