Shilin Night Market

Taipei’s most iconic night market—food, games, and an endless night-time buzz.

About Shilin Night Market

Introduction

Shilin Night Market is one of the biggest and most famous night markets in Taiwan. Located in the Shilin District of Taipei, it is known for its lively atmosphere, endless street food, and maze-like alleys filled with vendors, shops, and game stalls.

Historical Background

The market traces its origins back to the early 1900s, beginning as the Shilin daytime market near Cicheng Temple. Night market activities began forming around 1913 and expanded throughout the surrounding streets. Today, Shilin has evolved into a major entertainment and culinary destination that attracts millions of visitors every year.

Nearest MRT: Jiantan Station (Red Line) — Exit 1 leads directly to the main entrance.

Must-Try Foods

Chicken Cutlet (Recommended: Try Both Versions!)

Shilin’s famous chicken cutlet comes in two irresistible styles:
- The classic version is deep-fried to a golden, crispy finish.
- Some stalls grill it over charcoal after frying, adding smoky aroma and caramelized edges.

Both versions taste completely different—try both for the full experience!

Sausage in Sticky Rice

A Taiwanese street food icon also known as “big sausage wrapped in small sausage.” A grilled sticky rice bun is sliced open and stuffed with a juicy pork sausage.

Served with garlic, pickles, sweet soy, or chili sauce — chewy, smoky, and incredibly satisfying.

Oyster Omelet

A classic Taiwanese night market dish with fresh oysters, egg, and a chewy batter. Topped with the famous sweet–savory sauce that defines its flavor.

Many stalls specialize in this dish — follow the crowds to find the best one.

Stinky Tofu (Fried & Stewed)

Shilin offers both major styles of Taiwan’s infamous stinky tofu:
• Fried — crispy outside, tender inside, served with pickled cabbage.
• Stewed — simmered in a rich broth, softer and more pungent.

A must-try for adventurous eaters — the smell is strong, but the flavor wins many fans.

Visiting Tips

How to Make the Most of Your Visit

Shilin Night Market gets busy, especially on weekends and holidays. If you want to avoid the biggest crowds, aim to arrive in the early evening (around 5~6 pm) or later at night after 9:00 pm. Most food stalls stay open until around midnight.

Start from Jiantan Station Exit 1 (Not Shilin Station!), follow the crowd toward the main entrance, and slowly work your way through side alleys. Try sharing dishes with friends so you can sample more stalls without getting full too quickly. Wear comfortable shoes—the alleys can be narrow, slightly uneven, and you might end up walking more than you expect.

Be prepared for strong smells (like stinky tofu), lots of sizzling grills, and game booths with claw machines and carnival-style games. If you prefer a more relaxed vibe, visit on a weekday night and avoid typhoon days or heavy rain when some outdoor stalls may close early.

Practical Information

  • Location: Shilin District, Taipei City, near Jiantan MRT Station (Red Line).
  • Opening Hours: Generally late afternoon to around midnight; peak time is 7–10 pm.
  • Best Time to Visit: Weekday evenings for fewer crowds; weekends are the most lively but also the most crowded.
  • Payment: Many stalls are cash-only. Mobile payments such as LINE Pay or JKoPay are increasingly accepted, but credit cards are still uncommon—bring enough cash just in case.
  • Nearest MRT: Jiantan Station (Red Line), Exit 1. Follow the pedestrian flow across the street to reach the main entrance.
