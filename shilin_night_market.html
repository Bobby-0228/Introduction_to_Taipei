Shilin Night Market

Taipei’s most iconic night market—food, culture, and endless energy.

About Shilin Night Market

Introduction

Shilin Night Market is one of the biggest and most famous night markets in Taiwan. Located in the Shilin District of Taipei, it is known for its lively atmosphere, endless street food, and maze-like alleys filled with vendors, shops, and game stalls.

Historical Background

The market traces its origins back to the early 1900s, beginning as the Shilin daytime market near Cicheng Temple. Night market activities began forming around 1913 and expanded throughout the surrounding streets. Today, Shilin has evolved into a major entertainment and culinary destination that attracts millions of visitors every year.

Nearest MRT: Jiantan Station (Red Line) — Exit 1 leads directly to the main entrance.

Must-Try Foods

Large Fried Chicken Cutlet

A Shilin classic—oversized, crispy fried chicken cutlet seasoned with pepper or chili. One of the most iconic foods in the entire market.

Taiwanese Rice Sausage

“Big sausage wrapped in small sausage”—a grilled sticky rice bun wrapped around Taiwanese pork sausage, served with garlic and sweet soy sauce.

Oyster Omelet

A Taiwanese staple made with oysters, egg, starch batter, and a signature sweet-salty sauce. Rich, chewy, and full of flavor.

Flame-Grilled Beef Cubes

Fresh beef cubes torched to perfection. Often seasoned with sea salt, garlic, or black pepper for a smoky, juicy bite.
