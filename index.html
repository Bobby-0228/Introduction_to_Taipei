Modern skyscrapers nestled next to traditional temples. Michelin stars next to night markets.

The Ultimate Guide to Taipei

With 2.6 million people, Taipei is an amazing destination in Northern Taiwan. Whether you are looking for the world's tallest buildings or natural hot springs, this guide covers the 13 essential things you need to know.

Orientation

West Taipei (Old City)

Narrow lanes, alleyways, and history. Home to the Zhongzheng District, Taipei Main Station, and the Presidential Office.

East Taipei (Modern)

Wide grid streets, classy malls, and skyscrapers. Home to the Xinyi District and the iconic Taipei 101.

Getting In & Around

Taoyuan International (TPE)

The main international hub. 37km from city center. Take the Airport MRT (37 mins, ~160 NTD) or a Taxi (~1 hr, ~1500 NTD).

Songshan Airport (TSA)

Mostly domestic/Japan flights. Located right in the city center (20 mins from Main Station).

The Subway System

Clean, fast, efficient. Buy an Easy Card (100 NTD) at vending machines to tap in/out. Note: No eating or drinking permitted on the MRT.

Taxis & Walking

Taxis are metered and honest. Have your destination written in Chinese. Taipei is very walkable, but watch out for scooters parked on sidewalks!

Food & Night Markets

"People in Taipei love their food. Follow your nose!"

Night Markets

The heart of Taipei nightlife. Try Shilin (Biggest), Raohe (Best Layout), or Lehua (Local).

Must Try: Stinky Tofu

You'll smell it before you see it. Try it fried or with french fries to start!

Tea Culture

From milk tea to Oolong. Tea shops are on every corner (like TPT or the Oolong Tea Project).

Payment Tip

Pay at the counter when leaving sit-down restaurants. 10% service charge is common.

Popular Places

  • Taipei 101 - Formerly world's tallest. Great observatory and mall.
  • Elephant Mountain - Hike here for the best view of Taipei 101.
  • Shilin Night Markeet - Snacks.
  • National Palace Museum - World's best collection of Chinese artifacts (Jade Cabbage!).
  • Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall - Grand architecture and changing of the guards.
  • Taipei Zoo & Maokong Gondola - Pandas, cheap entry, and a scenic cable car ride for tea.
  • Dihua Street - Historic 1600s street perfect for traditional shopping.
