YangMingShan National Park

Volcanic landscapes, flower seasons, and cool mountain air just north of Taipei.

About Yangmingshan National Park

Introduction

Yangmingshan National Park, located just north of central Taipei, is one of Taiwan’s most beloved natural escapes. Known for its rolling grasslands, volcanic peaks, hot springs, and ever-changing seasonal scenery, it offers a refreshing contrast to the city’s busy streets.

Sitting atop the Tatun Volcano Group, the park features steaming fumaroles, sulfur vents, and dramatic mountain views, alongside tranquil hiking trails, flower fields, and quiet mountain villages. It’s one of the easiest places to experience Taiwan’s wild side in a single day trip from Taipei.

Landscape & Background

Historically known as Grass Mountain due to its windswept grassy slopes, Yangmingshan was renamed in the mid-20th century in honor of the Ming dynasty philosopher Wang Yangming. The area’s geology is shaped by the Tatun Volcano Group, a still-active volcanic system that gives rise to fumaroles, hot springs, and sulfur vents scattered throughout the park.

Designated as a national park in 1985, Yangmingshan protects a rich mix of subtropical and temperate ecosystems. Its terrain ranges from forested valleys to open grasslands and steep volcanic peaks. The park is also an important ecological research area, home to diverse bird species, butterflies, and unique highland flora.

Explore Yangmingshan

Xiaoyoukeng

Xiaoyoukeng, located in Yangmingshan National Park, is one of Taiwan’s most striking volcanic sites. Situated on the western slope of Mt. Qixing, it is famous for its dramatic geothermal landscape created by the Tatun Volcano Group. The area features active fumaroles, sulfur vents, steaming cliffs, and deposits of bright yellow sulfur crystals, giving visitors a rare, close-up look at ongoing volcanic activity.

Qingtiangang Grassland

Qingtiangang Grassland, located in Yangmingshan National Park, is a vast lava terrace formed by ancient volcanic activity. The area is known for its wide-open meadows, gently rolling hills, and peaceful atmosphere that feels far removed from the city. One of its most charming features is the presence of free-grazing cattle, which have become a well-loved symbol of the landscape.

Mt. Qixing

Mt. Qixing, located in Yangmingshan National Park, is the highest peak in Taipei at 1,120 meters. Formed by the Tatun Volcano Group, the mountain is known for its striking volcanic landscape, including fumaroles, sulfur vents, and rich geothermal activity. Well-marked hiking trails lead visitors through lush forests, grassy ridges, and areas of steaming volcanic terrain.

Visiting Tips

How to Make the Most of Your Visit

Weather in Yangmingshan can be cooler and windier than in downtown Taipei, especially in winter and at higher elevations. Bring a light jacket, comfortable walking shoes, and rain gear if the forecast is unstable. Fog is common, so don’t be surprised if the scenery changes dramatically within minutes.

On weekends and during flower seasons, popular areas like Zhuzihu and Xiaoyoukeng can get crowded. Visiting on weekday mornings usually offers a more relaxed experience. If you plan to hike, check route times in advance and keep an eye on the daylight, especially in winter when sunset comes early.

Practical Information

  • Location: North of Taipei City, within Beitou and Shilin districts
  • Suggested Time: Half-day to full day (longer if you enjoy hiking)
  • Best Seasons: Late winter to spring for flowers; autumn for silvergrass and clear views
  • Facilities: Visitor centers, restrooms, parking areas, a few cafes and small restaurants
  • Transport: Buses from MRT Jiantan, Shilin, Beitou, or Shipai stations connect directly to major park spots.
