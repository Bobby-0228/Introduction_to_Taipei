Historical Background

The museum‚Äôs core collection originated from the imperial palaces in Beijing, especially the Forbidden City. During the turbulent years of the early 20th century, these treasures were relocated multiple times for safekeeping. Eventually, a significant portion was transferred to Taiwan, and the National Palace Museum in Taipei officially opened in 1965.

Today, the museum stands as a major cultural landmark in East Asia, combining traditional aesthetics with modern exhibition design and conservation technology.

Nearest MRT: Shilin Station (Red Line), then take a short bus or taxi ride to the museum entrance.

Jadeite Cabbage

The Jadeite Cabbage is one of the most beloved pieces in the National Palace Museum. It uses the natural green and white colors of the jade to create realistic cabbage leaves and stalks, with a locust and katydid hidden among the leaves.

Mao Gong Ding

The Mao Gong Ding is a large bronze ritual tripod from the Western Zhou dynasty. It is renowned for having the longest inscription found on any ancient Chinese bronze, recording royal commands and political events of the time.
